The Web Research Group at Illinois Tech

About

The Web Research Group at Illinois Tech is a team of faculty and graduate students—as well as a few exceptional undergraduate students—researching web design and development from applied, theoretical, and pedagogical perspectives.

Please direct any questions or requests for more information to The Web Research Group’s director, Professor Karl Stolley at kstolley@iit.edu.

Karl Stolley, Director

Karl Stolley is an associate professor of information technology and management who researches both server- and client-side applications of established and emerging web standards. On the server side, he is interested in minimalist frameworks for lightweight development, data serialization formats, and the design of data APIs. On the client side, he focuses primarily on accessibility, including as achieved through progressive web applications. He also has a strong interest in interface and visual design, especially regarding web typography. A routine contributor to Mozilla Developer Network’s documentation, interactive code examples, and compatibility data, Stolley is enthusiastic about both studying and writing documentation. He is currently developing a book about antipatterns in HTML, an article on accessibility advances in the HTML and ARIA in HTML specifications, and two article-length surveys of web research in CS/IT over the last decade: one covering web-specific applied and theoretical research, and the other covering advances in web curriculum development and instruction.
